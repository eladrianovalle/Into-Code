About First Learning to Code

Saturday, March 07, 2015

We all have to start somewhere. This isn't the beginning. It started somewhere else, in another time, but here I am now. This part of my journey began exactly 2 years ago this month ( I was surprised to learn today! ) on Codecademy, with me running through their HTML, CSS, and JavaScript tracks.

As I often do, I started at the finish line, diving into JavaScript first; it was like diving into concrete. I mean, I got through the exercises, enjoying the acheivements; I decoded each challenge, akin to solving a puzzle in a video game, using my wits to unlock the next stage, but without retaining any knowledge. I wasn't 'getting' it. It was too foreign to me.

I finally got through JavaScript, winded after the struggle, and decided to take their HTML & CSS track next. This seemed easier. It brought me back to other beginnings, college days in the late 90s spent tinkering with Geocities pages, embedding images and midi files, creating garish inline styles to 'jazz' up my site. I knew this stuff, or at least the concepts. It got more fun.

Now I felt like I was starting to understand. It wasn't all gibberish. I was living the campfire montage in The 13th Warrior, in which Antonio Banderas learns the language of the Vikings ( turned out to be English! ). Out of the haze, objects came into focus. The tumblers were clicking into place, understanding soon to be unlocked. Or so I thought.

The next track I embarked upon was jQuery. I hadn't really understood JavaScript, but as I went through the lessons in jQuery, the operations became clearer, it was easier to see what was going on. With simpler logic on the surface, the structures became more apparent.

Yet upon completion of these 3 tracks, although I felt more knowledgeable in talking about these concepts, I still hadn't gained much perspective on how to use these concepts outside of their sandbox. I needed something more...
